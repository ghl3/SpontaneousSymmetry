Bio:

I'm a graduate student in High Energy Experimental Particle Physics at New York University. I work on an experiment called ATLAS, which is one of the general purpose experiments working on the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at CERN, an international laboratory located just outside Geneva. ATLAS is a giant particle detector, the biggest and most sophisticated of its kind. The ATLAS detector uses many different mechanisms to observe and record the proton collisions provided by LHC, and scientists like myself study those collisions to reach a better understanding of fundamental physics.
