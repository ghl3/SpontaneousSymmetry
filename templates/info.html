ABOUT ME

I'm a data scientist and computer programmer, and I am currently the head of Risk and Analytics at LendUp, an online lending startup. My job is to design, train, implement, and maintain our company's risk models, which use statistical modeling and machine learning to determine what credit products customers are eligible for. In addition, my job is to study our comany's data and to use it answer questions about our customers, our business goals, and to drive product decisions.

I attended the Insight Data Science Fellows Program, a selective and intensive two month course designed to cultivate analytic skills and help its students understand the many roles that a data scientist can play in a modern tech company.

I have a PhD in High Energy Experimental Particle Physics that I earned at from New York University. As a graduate student, I worked on work on an experiment called ATLAS, which works with the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at CERN, an international laboratory located just outside Geneva. As a member of the ATLAS collaboration, I built software to generate complicated statistical models, thereby enabling my collaborators to better understand the huge datasets being produced by our experiment.
